Understanding the Procedure

What Is All-on-X?

All-on-X (also known as All-on-4) is a full-arch dental implant procedure that replaces an entire set of upper or lower teeth with a fixed, permanent prosthesis anchored by four to six dental implants. Unlike traditional dentures that sit on top of your gums and can slip, click, or cause sore spots, All-on-X implants are surgically placed into your jawbone and function like natural teeth.

For patients who have lost most or all of their teeth — or whose remaining teeth are failing — All-on-X offers a life-changing solution: a complete, beautiful set of teeth that you never remove, that let you eat anything you want, and that look and feel like the teeth you were born with.

The procedure is typically completed in a single day. You walk in with failing or missing teeth. You walk out with a full set of new ones.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most patients with missing or failing teeth are candidates. Even patients told they don't have enough bone may be candidates thanks to modern techniques like zygomatic implants and bone grafting. The only way to know for sure is a consultation with Dr. Phillips.

Pricing varies depending on case complexity, number of implants, and prosthesis type. We provide transparent pricing at your consultation with no hidden fees. We are typically significantly less expensive than corporate implant centers while delivering the same or better clinical outcomes.

Unfortunately, the VA does not currently cover full-arch implant procedures, even when medically necessary. However, we work with veterans to find financing solutions that make this treatment accessible regardless of VA coverage.

With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime. The prosthesis typically lasts 15–20+ years before needing replacement. Regular check-ups and good oral hygiene are key to long-term success.

Most patients report significantly less discomfort than expected. The procedure is performed under IV sedation, so you're comfortable throughout. Post-operative discomfort is typically managed with over-the-counter pain medication within a few days.

For Our Veterans

Veteran-Owned. Veteran-Focused.
When the VA Says No, We Say Yes.

Dr. Matthew Phillips served 10 years in the United States Army as a dental officer, including combat deployments to Iraq and South Korea. He knows what it's like to serve, and he knows what it's like to navigate the VA system — the long wait times, the limited options, and the frustrating bureaucracy that often stands between a veteran and the care they've earned.

That's why he founded 3rd Set Smiles as a VA Community Care Provider. When the VA refers veterans to our practice, we treat them with the respect, urgency, and clinical excellence they deserve. And when the VA can't or won't provide the care a veteran needs — we find a way to make it happen anyway.

The reality is this: the Phoenix VA's implant board can take six months or longer just to determine if a veteran is a candidate for a single implant. Full-arch reconstruction? The VA almost never approves it. Veterans with failing teeth, chronic pain, and an inability to eat properly are being told to wait — or told no.

We believe veterans deserve better. If you've been told the VA can't help you, or you've been waiting months for an answer that may never come, call us. Dr. Phillips will evaluate your case personally, give you honest answers, and work with you to get the care you need now.

VA Dental Benefits We Accept

Delta Dental Federal (VA Community Care referrals), general dental exams, cleanings, restorative work, crowns, extractions, periodontal treatment, dentures, and emergency care. For procedures the VA does not cover, including full-arch implants, we offer flexible financing options.
